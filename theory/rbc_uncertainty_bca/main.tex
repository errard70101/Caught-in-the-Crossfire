\begin{abstract}
This note rewrites the uncertainty-accounting exercise in the classical
Business Cycle Accounting (BCA) prototype model. The model has no price
rigidity, no Phillips curve, and no monetary policy rule. The primitive
equilibrium conditions are a production function, a resource constraint, a
labor intratemporal condition, a capital Euler equation, and capital
accumulation. The only primitive source of second-moment variation is stochastic
volatility in total factor productivity. The goal is to clarify how TFP
uncertainty maps into the four canonical BCA wedges: efficiency, government or
resource, labor, and investment.
\end{abstract}
